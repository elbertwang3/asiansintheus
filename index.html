Mapping Asian-Americans in the U.S.


Asian-Americans are the fastest growing racial demographic in the U.S. Since 2000, the number of Asians in the U.S. has nearly doubled to about 20 million, hovering around 5-6% of the total population. The Asian-American population is mostly concentrated in metropolitan areas, and I personally grew up in one of these majority-Asian neighborhoods on the outskirts of Los Angeles County. Using data from the Census Bureau on the county and tract levels, I wanted to map the metropolitan areas where Asians are most heavily concentrated: Los Angeles County, San Francisco, and New York City.
